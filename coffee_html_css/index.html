The best coffee to drink at the morning is coffee without sugar, however with milk.


Which one type of coffee? You may ask. Let's read the article!

Latte

A mild, milky coffee in which espresso is softened by a large amount of steamed milk, so the coffee taste is gentle and smooth.

Cappuccino

More balanced and airy, because espresso, steamed milk, and foam are kept in equal proportion, giving it a stronger taste than a latte but a lighter texture.

Cortado

A small, stronger drink, where espresso and steamed milk are equal, so bitterness is reduced without making the drink very milky.

Flat White

A stronger and silkier milk coffee than a latte, because less milk is used and the microfoam is very fine, which lets the espresso stand out more.

Macchiato

Mostly espresso “marked” with only a little foamed milk, so it stays intense and concentrated.

Café con Leche

A coffee with hot milk in a more homely, comforting style, often less strict in proportion and depending more on local tradition.

No SUGAR?!

It is usually understood that sugar is separate, so a latte, cappuccino, cortado, flat white, or macchiato can be served unsweetened unless someone adds sugar, syrup, honey, or another sweetener. In some cafés, flavored versions are offered, but the standard form is normally taken to be without added sugar.
